Before your application can go live in a Game Warden DoW environment, it must receive two authorizations: an Authority to Operate (ATO) and a Game Warden Deployment Passport. This page explains what each is, how they relate, and the process for obtaining them.

## What Is an ATO?

An Authority to Operate (ATO) is a formal decision by a designated DoD Authorizing Official (AO) that a system is authorized to operate within a specific DoD environment for a defined period (typically 1–3 years). ATOs are issued after a rigorous security assessment under the Risk Management Framework (RMF).

For vendors deploying on Game Warden, the ATO process is significantly streamlined because:
- Game Warden itself holds a platform-level ATO
- Your application inherits a large number of security controls from the GW platform
- Second Front's security team guides you through the assessment process and interfaces with the AO on your behalf

## What Is a Deployment Passport?

A Deployment Passport is a Game Warden-specific artifact that serves as evidence your application has satisfied all of GW's technical and security requirements and has received authorization to deploy. It is issued by Second Front after your ATO is granted.

The Deployment Passport includes:
- Your application name and version
- The authorized environment (IL4 or IL5)
- The ATO expiration date
- Any conditions or limitations on the authorization
- The list of approved container image digests

## The Authorization Process

<Steps>
  <Step n={1} title="Complete Body of Evidence">
    Work through all required BoE sections in App Central: System Security Plan (SSP), Configuration Management Plan (CMP), Incident Response Plan (IRP), and Continuous Monitoring Plan (ConMon). All sections must be marked complete before security review begins.
  </Step>
  <Step n={2} title="Pass Pipeline Security Scans">
    Your container images must pass Game Warden's automated security pipeline, including vulnerability scanning, SAST analysis, and image signing. Critical findings must be remediated; High findings require documented justification to proceed.
  </Step>
  <Step n={3} title="Security Review by Second Front">
    Second Front's security team conducts a manual review of your BoE and pipeline results. This typically takes 2–4 weeks.
  </Step>
  <Step n={4} title="ATO Package Submission">
    Second Front compiles your security package and submits it to the Authorizing Official. The AO review typically takes 2–6 weeks.
  </Step>
  <Step n={5} title="ATO Decision">
    The Authorizing Official issues one of three decisions: ATO Granted, ATO with Conditions, or Denial.
  </Step>
  <Step n={6} title="Deployment Passport Issued">
    Upon ATO grant, Second Front issues your Deployment Passport and activates your production environment.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Timeline Expectations

| Phase | Typical Duration |
|---|---|
| BoE completion (vendor-led) | 4–8 weeks |
| Security review (Second Front) | 2–4 weeks |
| ATO submission and AO review | 2–6 weeks |
| Deployment Passport issuance | 1–3 business days after ATO grant |
| **Total (typical)** | **8–18 weeks** |

<Callout type="tip">
  You can accelerate the process by starting your BoE early—before your pipeline scans are complete. The SSP in particular can be drafted in parallel with technical onboarding. Contact your Second Front representative to access the BoE template and guidance documents.
</Callout>

## ATO Maintenance

ATOs are not permanent. Your authorization must be maintained through continuous monitoring, annual reviews, and timely remediation of new findings. Failure to maintain compliance can result in suspension of your Deployment Passport.
