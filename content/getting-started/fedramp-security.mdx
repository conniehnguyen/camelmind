Maintaining a FedRAMP Moderate authorization requires ongoing security processes, including continuous monitoring, vulnerability management, incident reporting, and annual assessments.

## Continuous Monitoring

FedRAMP requires continuous monitoring (ConMon) of authorized systems. Game Warden handles platform-level ConMon for the infrastructure it manages. As a tenant, you are responsible for application-level ConMon activities:

- **Vulnerability scanning**: Game Warden's pipeline automatically scans your container images on each deployment. Review scan results within the SLA windows.
- **Configuration drift detection**: Ensure your application's configuration does not deviate from the approved baseline documented in your BoE.
- **Log review**: Review application logs in the GW Grafana/Kibana instance regularly for anomalies or indicators of compromise.

## Plan of Action and Milestones (POA&M)

A POA&M documents known vulnerabilities or control gaps and the plan to remediate them. You must maintain an up-to-date POA&M in App Central.

| Finding Severity | Maximum POA&M Duration |
|---|---|
| Critical | 30 days |
| High | 90 days |
| Medium | 180 days |
| Low | 365 days |

POA&M items must be updated monthly with current status. Game Warden's security team reviews your POA&M during quarterly check-ins.

## Incident Reporting

FedRAMP requires that security incidents be reported to US-CERT within specific timeframes:

1. **Notify Second Front immediately** — Contact your account representative or submit an urgent support ticket in App Central.
2. **Report to US-CERT within 1 hour** for incidents involving a breach of federal data.
3. **Preserve evidence** — Do not delete logs or artifacts related to the incident until instructed by the security team.
4. **Complete a formal Incident Report** in App Central within 72 hours of initial detection.

<Callout type="warning">
  Failure to report security incidents within required timeframes can result in suspension of your FedRAMP authorization. When in doubt, report early and report often.
</Callout>

## Annual Assessment

FedRAMP authorizations require an annual security assessment conducted by a FedRAMP-accredited Third-Party Assessment Organization (3PAO). Your annual assessment obligations include:

- Making application environments available for testing during the assessment window
- Providing updated BoE documentation reflecting the current state of your application
- Remediating any findings identified during the assessment within the required timeframes

Game Warden will notify you of the annual assessment schedule at least 60 days in advance.
