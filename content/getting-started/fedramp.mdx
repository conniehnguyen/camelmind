Game Warden's FedRAMP path allows software vendors to deploy on infrastructure that has received a FedRAMP Moderate Authority to Operate. By deploying through Game Warden, your application can inherit a significant portion of the FedRAMP Moderate control baseline, reducing the time and cost required to achieve your own authorization.

## Game Warden's FedRAMP Authorization

Game Warden holds a FedRAMP Moderate ATO. This authorization covers the platform infrastructure—compute, storage, networking, logging, and security tooling—that underpins all FedRAMP deployments. As a tenant on this platform, your application operates within the authorized boundary and can inherit the controls that GW has already implemented and documented.

This does not mean your application is automatically FedRAMP authorized. You are responsible for the application-layer controls not covered by the platform. However, inheriting the platform controls dramatically reduces the number of controls your organization must independently implement and assess.

## Who Is FedRAMP Deployment For?

The FedRAMP path is appropriate for:
- Vendors targeting federal civilian agencies (DHS, HHS, VA, Treasury, etc.)
- Vendors whose customers require FedRAMP Moderate authorization as a condition of contract
- Vendors handling unclassified federal data that does not require DoD impact levels
- Vendors who are pursuing their own FedRAMP authorization and want a compliant hosting environment

## Key Differences from DoW Deployment

| Dimension | FedRAMP Path | DoW Path |
|---|---|---|
| Target customer | Federal civilian agencies | DoD program offices |
| Data classification | Unclassified | CUI / CDI (IL4/IL5) |
| Authorization framework | FedRAMP Moderate | DoD RMF / ATO |
| Network | Commercial internet (with controls) | NIPRNET |
| CAC/PIV required | Optional | Required at IL5 |
| Deployment Passport | Not applicable | Required |

## Getting Started

<Steps>
  <Step step="1" title="Create Your Account">
    Register at the Game Warden portal and select FedRAMP as your deployment path. See [Create Your Account](/getting-started/create-account) for step-by-step instructions.
  </Step>
  <Step step="2" title="Set Up Your FedRAMP Workspace">
    Complete organization setup and link your account to a FedRAMP workspace. See [FedRAMP Account Setup](/getting-started/fedramp-account-setup).
  </Step>
  <Step step="3" title="Review Entrance Criteria">
    Ensure your application meets all technical and security entrance criteria. See [Entrance Criteria](/getting-started/entrance-criteria).
  </Step>
  <Step step="4" title="Submit Your Application">
    Register your application in App Central, upload container images, and initiate the pipeline scan.
  </Step>
  <Step step="5" title="Complete Compliance Documentation">
    Work through the FedRAMP-specific compliance checklist in App Central, including your Customer Responsibility Matrix (CRM) and system description.
  </Step>
  <Step step="6" title="Security Review and Deployment">
    Second Front reviews your submission. Upon approval, your application is deployed to the FedRAMP environment.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="note">
  If your application will eventually need DoD IL4 or IL5 authorization, consider starting with the DoW path directly. Migrating between paths after deployment requires re-onboarding.
</Callout>
