Onboarding to Game Warden is a structured process designed to ensure your application meets all security and operational requirements before it goes live in a DoD or federal environment. This page provides an overview of the five onboarding phases, typical timelines, and who is involved at each stage.

## Onboarding Journey

```
Account Setup → Entrance Criteria Review → App Submission → Security Review → Deployment
```

### Phase 1: Account Setup (1–5 business days)

**What happens:** You create your Game Warden account, verify your organization, set up MFA, and select your deployment path (DoW, FedRAMP, or Commercial). Your Second Front account representative is assigned and conducts an initial kick-off call.

**Who is involved:** Vendor team (typically a tech lead or DevOps engineer), Second Front account representative.

**Key outputs:** Active GW account, verified organization, deployment path selected.

See [Create Your Account](/getting-started/create-account) for detailed instructions.

### Phase 2: Entrance Criteria Review (5–14 business days)

**What happens:** You review the entrance criteria for your deployment path and remediate any gaps. You submit your application in App Central, and the Second Front onboarding team conducts the entrance criteria review. Any issues are reported as findings you must address before proceeding.

**Who is involved:** Vendor engineering team, Second Front onboarding team.

**Key outputs:** Entrance criteria approval, application registered in App Central.

See [Entrance Criteria](/getting-started/entrance-criteria) for the full requirements.

### Phase 3: App Submission and Pipeline Scan (3–7 business days)

**What happens:** You push your container images to the Game Warden Harbor registry and trigger the CI/CD pipeline. The pipeline runs automated security scans (SAST, vulnerability scanning, image signing). Critical findings block progression; High findings require documented justification.

**Who is involved:** Vendor engineering team, GW pipeline automation.

**Key outputs:** Clean pipeline scan (or documented justification for accepted findings), signed container images in the GW registry.

See [Pipelines and Tooling](/platform/pipelines) for details on the pipeline process.

### Phase 4: Security Review (10–30 business days)

**What happens:** You work on your Body of Evidence (BoE) package, documenting your application's security posture, system boundary, and control implementation. Second Front's security team reviews the BoE, pipeline results, and any outstanding findings. For DoW deployments, this phase culminates in ATO submission.

**Who is involved:** Vendor tech lead and security representative, Second Front security team, DoD Authorizing Official (for DoW/ATO path).

**Key outputs:** Completed BoE, security review approval, ATO (for DoW path), Deployment Passport (for DoW path).

See [Body of Evidence](/gw-app/boe) and [ATO and Deployment Passport](/getting-started/dow-deployment-passport) for details.

### Phase 5: Deployment (1–3 business days)

**What happens:** Your application is deployed to the target Game Warden environment. Second Front provisions your production namespace, configures ingress and service mesh policies, and hands off operational access to your team. A final validation check confirms the application is running as expected.

**Who is involved:** Vendor engineering team, Second Front platform team.

**Key outputs:** Live application in production environment, operational access handed to vendor.

## Timeline Summary

| Phase | Typical Duration |
|---|---|
| Account Setup | 1–5 business days |
| Entrance Criteria Review | 5–14 business days |
| App Submission and Scan | 3–7 business days |
| Security Review | 10–30 business days |
| Deployment | 1–3 business days |
| **Total (DoW path)** | **20–59 business days** |
| **Total (Commercial path)** | **10–29 business days** |

<Callout type="tip">
  You can compress the timeline significantly by starting your BoE documentation during the Entrance Criteria phase rather than waiting until Security Review begins. Contact your Second Front representative for the BoE template as soon as your account is set up.
</Callout>
