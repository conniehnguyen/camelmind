Impact Level 5 (IL5) is the most stringent deployment tier Game Warden supports. It covers National Security System (NSS) data and higher-sensitivity Controlled Unclassified Information. If your program has been designated IL5 by your Authorizing Official, you must meet all IL4 requirements plus the additional controls described on this page.

## What Makes IL5 Different from IL4

IL5 builds on the IL4 baseline and adds controls in four key areas:

1. **Personnel security** — additional vetting for individuals with access to IL5 systems
2. **Cryptographic standards** — NSA-approved cryptography required for data at rest and in transit
3. **Network isolation** — stricter segmentation requirements; IL5 workloads may not share network paths with IL4 workloads
4. **Physical security** — data must reside in facilities that meet DoD Tier III+ data center standards

## Additional Compliance Requirements

### Authentication

- CAC/PIV authentication is required for all human access to IL5 systems. Username/password authentication alone is not sufficient.
- Service accounts must use certificate-based mutual TLS (mTLS). Shared secrets or API keys without additional controls are not permitted.
- Sessions must enforce re-authentication after 15 minutes of inactivity for privileged users.

### Encryption

- Data at rest must be encrypted using FIPS 140-2 validated modules (Level 2 or higher).
- Data in transit must use TLS 1.2 or higher with NSA Suite B or Commercial National Security Algorithm (CNSA) Suite cipher suites.
- Key management must use a FIPS-validated key management solution. Game Warden provides a managed secrets service that meets this requirement.

### Network Controls

- IL5 workloads are deployed in isolated namespaces with no shared ingress paths with IL4 workloads.
- All external traffic must traverse Game Warden's IL5-specific ingress controller, which enforces CAC authentication at the perimeter.
- Outbound traffic requires explicit allowlist rules; default-deny egress policies are enforced.

### Audit Logging

- All access events, authentication events, and privileged actions must be logged to the GW SIEM with tamper-evident controls.
- Log retention for IL5 is a minimum of 3 years.
- Logs must include user identity, timestamp, action taken, and outcome.

### Supply Chain

- All container images must be sourced from Iron Bank (the DoD's hardened container registry). Vendor-built images must be layered on an approved Iron Bank base image.
- Software Bill of Materials (SBOM) must be submitted with each image version.

## Steps to Achieve IL5 Compliance on Game Warden

<Steps>
  <Step n={1} title="Confirm IL5 Designation">
    Obtain written confirmation from your DoD program office that IL5 is required. This is typically documented in your contract or Memorandum of Agreement (MOA).
  </Step>
  <Step n={2} title="Notify Your Second Front Representative">
    Contact your Second Front account team to request IL5 onboarding. IL5 environments are provisioned separately and require additional coordination.
  </Step>
  <Step n={3} title="Configure CAC/PIV Authentication">
    Work with the GW team to integrate your application with the IL5 identity provider, which enforces CAC/PIV at login. Game Warden provides a pre-configured OIDC integration for this purpose.
  </Step>
  <Step n={4} title="Validate Encryption Configuration">
    Ensure your application uses FIPS-validated cryptographic libraries. Game Warden will run an encryption posture check as part of the IL5 security review.
  </Step>
  <Step n={5} title="Source Images from Iron Bank">
    Update your container build pipeline to use Iron Bank base images. Submit your SBOM as part of your BoE package.
  </Step>
  <Step n={6} title="Complete IL5-Specific BoE Sections">
    The IL5 BoE includes additional control documentation sections beyond the standard IL4 BoE. Your Second Front representative will provide the IL5-specific BoE template.
  </Step>
  <Step n={7} title="Undergo IL5 Security Review">
    The IL5 security review includes additional testing: penetration testing, cryptographic validation, and network isolation verification. Plan for an additional 2–4 weeks compared to the IL4 review timeline.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning">
  IL5 onboarding timelines are typically 30–60 days longer than IL4. Factor this into your program schedule and communicate requirements to your program office early.
</Callout>
