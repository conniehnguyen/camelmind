Game Warden supports three deployment paths, each designed for a different compliance posture and customer type. Before onboarding your application, you must select the path that matches your target environment. Your choice determines which security controls apply, what authorization artifacts you need to produce, and which Game Warden infrastructure hosts your workload.

## Deployment Paths at a Glance

| Environment | Classification Level | Auth Framework | Typical Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| **DoW (Department of War)** | IL4 / IL5 (NIPRNET, SIPRNET-adjacent) | DoD RMF / ATO | 60–120 days |
| **FedRAMP** | Unclassified (Low / Moderate) | FedRAMP Authorization | 30–90 days |
| **Commercial** | Unclassified / Commercial | SOC2 / ISO 27001 | 7–30 days |

## Path Descriptions

### DoW (DoD-Aligned Deployment)

The DoW path is for software vendors whose applications will run on Department of Defense infrastructure at Impact Level 4 or Impact Level 5. This path requires a formal Authority to Operate (ATO) and the completion of a Body of Evidence (BoE) package. Game Warden's DoW environments are hosted on Platform One–aligned infrastructure with NIPRNET connectivity.

Choose DoW if:
- Your customer is a DoD program office or military branch
- Your application handles Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI) or Covered Defense Information (CDI)
- You are targeting IL4 or IL5 environments
- You need a Deployment Passport to enter the DoD software ecosystem

### FedRAMP

The FedRAMP path is for vendors targeting federal civilian agencies or DoD components that accept FedRAMP Moderate authorization as sufficient. Game Warden maintains a FedRAMP Moderate Authorization to Operate, which your application can inherit controls from, significantly reducing your compliance burden.

Choose FedRAMP if:
- Your customers are federal civilian agencies (DHS, HHS, VA, etc.)
- Your data classification is Unclassified
- You need to inherit a FedRAMP Moderate control baseline
- Your application is cloud-hosted and does not touch classified networks

### Commercial

The Commercial path is for vendors that need a secure, compliant deployment environment but are not yet targeting classified DoD workloads. This path offers the fastest time-to-deploy and is appropriate for vendors in early-stage government contracting or those operating in commercial cloud environments with compliance requirements (SOC2, ISO 27001).

Choose Commercial if:
- Your application is in pre-production or early pilot with a government customer
- You do not yet have a DoD contract requiring IL4/IL5
- You need a secure, monitored deployment environment with a lower compliance overhead
- You are evaluating Game Warden before committing to a DoW or FedRAMP path

## How to Choose

If you are unsure which path applies to your program, start by answering these questions:

1. **What is the classification level of the data your application will process?** If CUI or CDI, you need DoW. If unclassified but federal, FedRAMP. If commercial, Commercial.
2. **Does your contract reference a specific Impact Level?** If yes, that determines your path.
3. **Is your customer a DoD program office?** If yes, DoW is almost certainly required.
4. **Are you in active contract negotiations?** If you have not yet received a contract, Commercial is the right starting point.

<Callout type="tip">
  If your program spans multiple environments (e.g., a commercial instance for demos and a DoW instance for production), you can run separate deployments on different paths. Contact your Second Front account representative to discuss multi-environment strategies.
</Callout>

<Callout type="important">
  Switching paths after onboarding requires re-evaluation of your entrance criteria and may reset your BoE progress. Select the correct path before beginning onboarding.
</Callout>
