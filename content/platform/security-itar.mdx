The International Traffic in Arms Regulations (ITAR) govern the export of defense-related articles, services, and technical data. If your application handles ITAR-controlled technical data, you must ensure your deployment on Game Warden meets ITAR requirements.

## What Is ITAR?

ITAR is administered by the U.S. Department of State, Directorate of Defense Trade Controls (DDTC). It controls the export and import of defense articles and services listed on the United States Munitions List (USML). For software vendors, ITAR is most relevant when your application:

- Stores, processes, or transmits defense technical data (e.g., weapon system specifications, military communications)
- Provides services related to the design, development, or operation of defense articles
- Is licensed by or used in conjunction with a DoD program involving USML items

## ITAR-Capable Environments

Game Warden's DoW IL4 and IL5 environments are designed to support ITAR-controlled workloads. Specifically:

- **Data residency**: All data is stored within the United States on U.S.-owned and operated infrastructure (AWS GovCloud)
- **Access control**: Access to ITAR-controlled data is restricted to U.S. Persons (U.S. citizens and permanent residents) by platform controls and policy
- **Personnel**: Second Front employees with access to IL4/IL5 infrastructure are U.S. Persons; the company maintains compliance records accordingly

<Callout type="important">
  Commercial and FedRAMP path deployments are **not** ITAR-capable. Do not store or process ITAR-controlled technical data in Commercial or FedRAMP environments.
</Callout>

## Vendor Responsibilities for ITAR Compliance

Game Warden provides the infrastructure controls, but vendors are responsible for ITAR compliance at the application layer:

### Access Controls

- Your application must restrict access to ITAR-controlled data to U.S. Persons only
- Implement authentication that verifies user identity and, where required, citizenship status (typically via CAC/PIV, which confirms U.S. Person status for DoD employees and contractors)
- Maintain access logs showing who accessed ITAR-controlled data and when

### Audit Logging

- All access to ITAR-controlled data within your application must be logged with user identity, timestamp, data accessed, and action taken
- Logs must be retained for the period required by your export license or DDTC agreement (typically 5 years)
- GW's logging infrastructure supports this requirement; ensure your application writes sufficient context to each log entry

### Export Control Documentation

- Maintain a current export control compliance plan documenting how ITAR requirements are addressed in your application
- Include this documentation in your BoE under the System Security Plan
- If your application has an export license from DDTC, attach it to your BoE submission

### Employee Training

- Ensure all employees with access to the Game Warden environment (including developers) have completed ITAR export control training
- Document training completion in your compliance records

## What Game Warden Does vs. What Vendors Must Do

| Control | Game Warden | Vendor |
|---|---|---|
| Data residency (U.S. only) | Yes — GovCloud infrastructure | Confirm no data sync to non-U.S. systems |
| U.S. Person access to infrastructure | Yes — GW staff are U.S. Persons | Implement U.S. Person verification in app |
| Audit logging infrastructure | Yes — Loki log aggregation | Write ITAR-relevant events to application logs |
| Access control to data | No — app layer | Implement role-based access to ITAR data |
| Export control documentation | No | Maintain and include in BoE |

If you have questions about ITAR applicability to your specific program, consult your legal counsel or your organization's export control officer before onboarding.
