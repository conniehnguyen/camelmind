When you encounter issues with the Game Warden platform, need guidance during onboarding, or have questions about findings or compliance requirements, submit a support ticket through App Central. This page explains when and how to open tickets, what information to include, and how escalation works.

## When to Open a Ticket

Open a support ticket for:
- Platform or pipeline issues (e.g., pipeline stuck, Harbor registry inaccessible)
- Questions about findings or remediation guidance
- BoE or compliance questions not covered in the documentation
- Account or access issues (e.g., unable to log in, role permissions incorrect)
- Requesting managed services (e.g., provisioning a PostgreSQL database)
- Escalating a finding SLA concern
- Reporting a suspected security incident

Do not use support tickets for:
- General application debugging unrelated to the GW platform
- Questions covered in this documentation (search first)
- Requesting features (use the feature request form in the portal instead)

## How to Submit a Ticket

<Steps>
  <Step step="1" title="Navigate to Support">
    In the Game Warden portal, click **Support** in the top navigation bar, then click **New Ticket**.
  </Step>
  <Step step="2" title="Select a Category">
    Choose the category that best matches your issue: Platform / Infrastructure, Onboarding, Findings & Security, Account & Access, Managed Services, Compliance & BoE, or Security Incident.
  </Step>
  <Step step="3" title="Fill In Ticket Details">
    Provide as much detail as possible:
    - **Application Name**: Select from the dropdown (links the ticket to your app's context)
    - **Environment**: DoW / FedRAMP / Commercial
    - **Priority**: Select P1 through P4 (see priority definitions below)
    - **Subject**: A concise summary of the issue
    - **Description**: Detailed description including steps to reproduce, expected vs. actual behavior, and any error messages
    - **Attachments**: Screenshots, log snippets, or relevant files (max 10MB per attachment)
  </Step>
  <Step step="4" title="Submit and Track">
    Click **Submit**. You will receive a confirmation email with a ticket number. Track ticket status in **Support > My Tickets**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What to Include in Your Ticket

A good ticket allows the support team to begin investigating without needing to ask follow-up questions. Include:

- **Affected application name and version**
- **Environment** (DoW IL4/IL5, FedRAMP, Commercial)
- **Timestamp** of when the issue was first observed
- **Steps to reproduce** (numbered, specific)
- **Expected behavior** vs. **actual behavior**
- **Error messages or logs** (paste relevant snippets; do not attach entire log files unless requested)
- **What you have already tried** to resolve the issue

## Priority Levels and SLA

| Priority | Definition | Initial Response | Resolution Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| **P1 – Critical** | Production system down; security incident; complete loss of access | 1 hour | 4 hours |
| **P2 – High** | Significant functionality impaired; pipeline blocked; SLA breach imminent | 4 hours | 1 business day |
| **P3 – Medium** | Partial impairment; workaround available | 1 business day | 5 business days |
| **P4 – Low** | General questions; documentation requests; non-urgent guidance | 2 business days | Best effort |

SLAs apply during Second Front's business hours (9am–6pm ET, Monday–Friday) unless a 24/7 support contract is in place.

## Escalation

If you believe a ticket is not being addressed with appropriate urgency:

1. **Reply to the ticket** with an escalation request explaining the business impact.
2. **Contact your Second Front account representative directly** for P1/P2 issues that have not received a response within the SLA window.
3. For security incidents, call the Second Front emergency security line provided in your onboarding documentation.

<Callout type="important">
  For suspected security incidents, do not wait for a ticket response. Contact your Second Front representative directly and simultaneously submit a P1 ticket with the Security Incident category selected.
</Callout>
