The Body of Evidence (BoE) is the core compliance documentation package that accompanies your application through the Game Warden security review and ATO process. It demonstrates that your application meets the security and operational requirements of the target DoD or federal environment.

<Callout type="important">
  All BoE sections are required. A BoE with incomplete sections cannot be submitted for security review. Start working on your BoE as early as possible—ideally in parallel with your technical onboarding steps.
</Callout>

## What Is a BoE?

A Body of Evidence is a collection of documents and attestations that describe your application's security posture, how security controls are implemented, and how you respond to security events. It is the primary artifact that the Second Front security team and the DoD Authorizing Official (AO) use to evaluate whether your application is authorized to operate.

The BoE structure follows the DoD Risk Management Framework (RMF) documentation requirements, but Game Warden provides templates and guidance that significantly reduce the effort compared to a standalone RMF package.

## Required BoE Sections

### System Security Plan (SSP)

The SSP is the foundational document of your BoE. It describes:
- What your application does and who uses it
- The system boundary (what is inside vs. outside the authorization boundary)
- The data types your application processes
- How each applicable security control is implemented (implemented by GW, inherited from GW, or vendor-implemented)

Game Warden provides a pre-populated SSP template that includes all controls inherited from the GW platform, significantly reducing the number of controls you must document independently.

### Configuration Management Plan (CMP)

The CMP describes how your organization manages changes to the application, including:
- Change control process (how changes are reviewed and approved)
- How container image versions are tracked and inventoried
- Procedures for emergency changes
- How configuration baselines are documented and maintained

### Incident Response Plan (IRP)

The IRP describes how your team will detect, respond to, and recover from security incidents. It must include:
- Incident classification criteria (what constitutes an incident)
- Notification procedures and escalation paths (including Second Front and US-CERT)
- Roles and responsibilities during an incident
- Evidence preservation procedures
- Post-incident review process

### Continuous Monitoring Plan (ConMon)

The ConMon plan describes how you will maintain security compliance after your ATO is granted, including how you will monitor for new vulnerabilities, track and remediate findings, and report status.

## Creating a BoE in App Central

<Steps>
  <Step n={1} title="Navigate to Your Application">
    In App Central, click on your application to open the detail view, then select the **BoE** tab.
  </Step>
  <Step n={2} title="Download the Templates">
    Click **Download Templates** to get the pre-populated templates for each BoE section. The templates include instructions and example content. Complete each template offline.
  </Step>
  <Step n={3} title="Upload Completed Documents">
    For each section, click the section name, then **Upload Document**. Accepted formats: DOCX, PDF. Maximum file size: 50MB per document.
  </Step>
  <Step n={4} title="Submit Section for Review">
    After uploading a document, click **Submit for Review** to notify the Second Front security team that the section is ready. You can submit sections incrementally—you do not need to wait until all sections are complete.
  </Step>
  <Step n={5} title="Respond to Reviewer Feedback">
    Reviewers may leave comments on submitted sections requesting clarification or changes. You will receive a notification when feedback is available. Update your document and re-upload to address feedback.
  </Step>
  <Step n={6} title="Track Overall Completion">
    The BoE dashboard shows overall completion percentage and the status of each section. All sections must reach "Complete" status before the security review can proceed to ATO submission.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## After BoE Submission

Once all BoE sections are marked Complete, Second Front's security team conducts a holistic review of the package. If everything is in order, your package moves to ATO submission. See [ATO and Deployment Passport](/getting-started/dow-deployment-passport) for what happens next.

<Callout type="tip">
  The SSP is typically the most time-consuming section. Request the SSP template on your first day of onboarding, even before your technical setup is complete. Many fields can be filled in while your engineers are completing technical requirements.
</Callout>
